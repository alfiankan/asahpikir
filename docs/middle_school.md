## 1. Ratios & Proportions

### Core Concept: Scaling and Constant Rates
- **Level 1 (Intuition)**: Mixing colors or ingredients in proportions.
- **Level 2 (Procedural)**: Solving unit rate problems and cross-multiplication.
- **Level 3 (Challenge)**: Direct vs. inverse proportions with double scales.

### Interactive Widget Idea: `RatioMixer`
* **Interaction**: Sliders for "Blue Paint" and "Yellow Paint". Shows resulting green shade and simplified ratio. User scales up/down to match a target shade.

### Graded Quiz Questions (At least 5 per level)

#### Level 1: Ratio Identification & Simplification
1. **Question 1**: What is the simplified form of the ratio 4:6?
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Write the ratio as a fraction: $4/6$.
     2. Find the Greatest Common Divisor (GCD) of 4 and 6, which is 2.
     3. Divide both numbers by 2: $4/2 = 2$ and $6/2 = 3$.
     4. The simplified ratio is $2:3$.
2. **Question 2**: Simplify the ratio 8:12.
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. GCF of 8 and 12 is 4.
     2. Divide both by 4: $8/4 = 2$ and $12/4 = 3$.
     3. Ratio is $2:3$.
3. **Question 3**: If a jar has 5 red marbles and 15 blue marbles, what is the ratio of red to blue marbles?
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Write the raw ratio: $5:15$.
     2. Divide by the GCF of 5: $5/5 = 1$ and $15/5 = 3$.
     3. Ratio is $1:3$.
4. **Question 4**: Simplify the ratio 10:25.
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. GCF is 5.
     2. Divide by 5: $10/5 = 2$ and $25/5 = 5$.
     3. Ratio is $2:5$.
5. **Question 5**: A basket contains 6 apples and 8 oranges. What is the ratio of apples to total fruit?
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Count total fruit: $6 + 8 = 14$.
     2. Write the ratio of apples to total: $6:14$.
     3. Divide by the GCF (2) to simplify: $3:7$.

#### Level 2: Direct Proportions & Cross-Multiplication
1. **Question 6**: Solve for $x$ in the proportion: $3/5 = x/15$.
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. In a proportion, the relationship between denominators must equal the relationship between numerators.
     2. Compare denominators: to go from 5 to 15, we multiply by 3.
     3. Multiply the numerator 3 by the same factor: $3 \times 3 = 9$.
     4. So, $x = 9$.
2. **Question 7**: Solve for $x$: $2/3 = 8/x$.
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Compare numerators: to go from 2 to 8, we multiply by 4.
     2. Multiply denominator 3 by the same factor: $3 \times 4 = 12$.
     3. So, $x = 12$.
3. **Question 8**: Solve for $x$: $5/8 = x/24$.
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Compare denominators: $24 / 8 = 3$.
     2. Multiply numerator: $5 \times 3 = 15$.
     3. $x = 15$.
4. **Question 9**: A recipe calls for 2 cups of sugar for every 5 cups of flour. If you use 15 cups of flour, how much sugar is needed?
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Set up the proportion: $2/5 = x/15$.
     2. Scale denominator: $15 / 5 = 3$.
     3. Multiply sugar scale: $2 \times 3 = 6$ cups of sugar.
5. **Question 10**: Solve for $x$: $4/7 = 16/x$.
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Scale numerator: $16 / 4 = 4$.
     2. Scale denominator: $7 \times 4 = 28$.
     3. $x = 28$.

#### Level 3: Inverse Proportions & Rate Analysis
1. **Question 11**: If 2 painters take 6 hours to paint a house, how long will it take 4 painters working at the same speed?
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Understand this is an inverse proportion: more painters mean *less* time.
     2. Calculate the total workload in worker-hours: $2 \text{ painters} \times 6 \text{ hours} = 12 \text{ worker-hours}$.
     3. Divide the workload by the new number of painters: $12 \text{ worker-hours} / 4 \text{ painters} = 3 \text{ hours}$.
2. **Question 12**: If 3 pumps take 8 hours to empty a pool, how long will it take 6 identical pumps?
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Calculate total pump-hours: $3 \times 8 = 24 \text{ hours}$.
     2. Divide by 6 pumps: $24 / 6 = 4 \text{ hours}$.
3. **Question 13**: A car travels at $60\text{ km/h}$ and takes 3 hours to arrive. How long does it take at $90\text{ km/h}$?
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Calculate distance: $60 \text{ km/h} \times 3 \text{ h} = 180 \text{ km}$.
     2. Divide by new speed: $180 \text{ km} / 90 \text{ km/h} = 2 \text{ hours}$.
4. **Question 14**: If 5 workers take 12 days to build a wall, how many days will 10 workers take?
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Calculate total worker-days: $5 \times 12 = 60 \text{ days}$.
     2. Divide by 10 workers: $60 / 10 = 6 \text{ days}$.
5. **Question 15**: If 4 identical machines pack 100 boxes in 5 hours, how long would it take 2 machines to pack the same number of boxes?
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Calculate machine-hours: $4 \times 5 = 20 \text{ hours}$.
     2. Divide by 2 machines: $20 / 2 = 10 \text{ hours}$.

## 2. Intro to Algebra

### Core Concept: Variables, Equations, and Balance
- **Level 1 (Intuition)**: Replacing weights on a scale with variable boxes $x$.
- **Level 2 (Procedural)**: Solving one-step and two-step linear equations ($ax + b = c$).
- **Level 3 (Challenge)**: Translating word problems into algebraic systems.

### Interactive Widget Idea: `AlgebraBalance`
* **Interaction**: Left and right side of an equation balance scale. User can perform identical operations to both sides to isolate the variable $x$.

### Graded Quiz Questions (At least 5 per level)

#### Level 1: One-Step Equations
1. **Question 1**: Solve for $x$: $x + 5 = 12$. What operation is needed first?
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Locate the variable $x$ on the left side of the equation.
     2. To isolate $x$, we must remove the $+ 5$.
     3. Perform the inverse of addition (subtraction): subtract 5 from both sides.
     4. $x = 12 - 5 \rightarrow x = 7$.
2. **Question 2**: Solve for $x$: $x - 3 = 8$.
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Undo the subtraction of 3.
     2. Add 3 to both sides: $x = 8 + 3$.
     3. $x = 11$.
3. **Question 3**: Solve for $x$: $4x = 20$.
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Undo multiplication by 4.
     2. Divide both sides by 4: $x = 20 / 4$.
     3. $x = 5$.
4. **Question 4**: Solve for $x$: $x / 3 = 6$.
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Undo division by 3.
     2. Multiply both sides by 3: $x = 6 \times 3$.
     3. $x = 18$.
5. **Question 5**: Solve for $x$: $x + 9 = 15$.
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Subtract 9 from both sides.
     2. $x = 15 - 9 = 6$.

#### Level 2: Two-Step Equations
1. **Question 6**: Solve for $x$: $3x + 2 = 14$.
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Isolate the variable term $3x$ first by removing addition/subtraction.
     2. Subtract 2 from both sides: $3x = 12$.
     3. Isolate $x$ by removing multiplication/division.
     4. Divide both sides by 3: $x = 4$.
2. **Question 7**: Solve for $x$: $2x - 5 = 11$.
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Add 5 to both sides: $2x = 16$.
     2. Divide by 2: $x = 8$.
3. **Question 8**: Solve for $x$: $5x + 3 = 23$.
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Subtract 3 from both sides: $5x = 20$.
     2. Divide by 5: $x = 4$.
4. **Question 9**: Solve for $x$: $x/2 - 4 = 1$.
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Add 4 to both sides: $x/2 = 5$.
     2. Multiply by 2: $x = 10$.
5. **Question 10**: Solve for $x$: $4x - 7 = 9$.
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Add 7 to both sides: $4x = 16$.
     2. Divide by 4: $x = 4$.

#### Level 3: Variables on Both Sides
1. **Question 11**: Solve for $x$: $2x + 3 = x + 8$.
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Gather all variable terms on one side. Subtract $x$ from both sides: $x + 3 = 8$.
     2. Gather constant numbers on the opposite side. Subtract 3 from both sides: $x = 5$.
2. **Question 12**: Solve for $x$: $3x - 4 = 2x + 5$.
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Subtract $2x$ from both sides: $x - 4 = 5$.
     2. Add 4 to both sides: $x = 9$.
3. **Question 13**: Solve for $x$: $5x + 2 = 2x + 11$.
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Subtract $2x$ from both sides: $3x + 2 = 11$.
     2. Subtract 2 from both sides: $3x = 9$.
     3. Divide by 3: $x = 3$.
4. **Question 14**: Solve for $x$: $4x - 6 = 2x + 8$.
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Subtract $2x$ from both sides: $2x - 6 = 8$.
     2. Add 6 to both sides: $2x = 14$.
     3. Divide by 2: $x = 7$.
5. **Question 15**: Solve for $x$: $x + 12 = 3x - 4$.
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Subtract $x$ from both sides: $12 = 2x - 4$.
     2. Add 4 to both sides: $16 = 2x$.
     3. Divide by 2: $x = 8$.

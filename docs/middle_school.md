# Middle School Curriculum (Grades 6-8) - AsahFikir

This document details the modules, interactive concepts, and adaptive quiz ideas for the **Middle School** track.

---

## 1. Number Systems (Integers, Rationals)

### Core Concept: The Number Line and Signed Operations
- **Level 1 (Intuition)**: Visualizing absolute value and signed operations as movements on a horizontal number line.
- **Level 2 (Procedural)**: Adding/subtracting negative values, understanding rational number placement.
- **Level 3 (Challenge)**: Signed inequality logic puzzles.

### Interactive Widget Idea: `NumberLineExplorer`
* **Interaction**: A number line slider. Dragging a point $A$ and point $B$ shows the distance $|A - B|$. Clicking buttons performs addition or subtraction steps visually.
* **Quiz**: "If you start at $-3$ and add $-4$, in which direction do you move, and what is the final coordinate?"

---

## 2. Ratios & Proportions

### Core Concept: Scaling and Constant Rates
- **Level 1 (Intuition)**: Mixing colors or ingredients in proportions.
- **Level 2 (Procedural)**: Solving unit rate problems and cross-multiplication.
- **Level 3 (Challenge)**: Direct vs. inverse proportions with double scales.

### Interactive Widget Idea: `RatioMixer`
* **Interaction**: Sliders for "Blue Paint" and "Yellow Paint". Shows resulting green shade and simplified ratio. User scales up/down to match a target shade.
* **Quiz**: "To get the perfect shade of green, you need 3 cups of blue for every 5 cups of yellow. If you have 15 cups of yellow, how many cups of blue do you need?"

---

## 3. Exponents & Roots

### Core Concept: Repeated Multiplication and Area/Volume Scaling
- **Level 1 (Intuition)**: Visual grid scaling representing $x^2$ (square area) and $x^3$ (cube volume).
- **Level 2 (Procedural)**: Rules of exponents ($x^a \cdot x^b = x^{a+b}$), calculating simple square/cube roots.
- **Level 3 (Challenge)**: Negative and fractional exponents.

### Interactive Widget Idea: `ExponentGrid`
* **Interaction**: Interactive grid scaling. Increasing the base changes the block dimensions, and increasing the power multiplies the blocks in 2D/3D spaces.
* **Quiz**: "If $2^3 = 8$ and $2^4 = 16$, what is the geometric representation of $2^5$? How many times larger is it than $2^3$?"

---

## 4. Intro to Algebra

### Core Concept: Variables, Equations, and Balance
- **Level 1 (Intuition)**: Replacing weights on a scale with variable boxes $x$.
- **Level 2 (Procedural)**: Solving one-step and two-step linear equations ($ax + b = c$).
- **Level 3 (Challenge)**: Translating word problems into algebraic systems.

### Interactive Widget Idea: `AlgebraBalance`
* **Interaction**: Left and right side of an equation balance scale. User can perform identical operations to both sides (e.g. "-3 from both sides" or "/2 on both sides") to isolate the variable $x$.
* **Quiz**: "Solve for $x$: $2x + 5 = 13$. Perform operations on the scale to isolate $x$."

---

## 5. Geometry

### Core Concept: Angles, Triangles, and Transformations
- **Level 1 (Intuition)**: Drag-and-drop angles to see relationships (supplementary, vertical).
- **Level 2 (Procedural)**: Calculating angles using properties of parallel lines intersected by a transversal.
- **Level 3 (Challenge)**: Pythagorean theorem visual proof (squares on sides of a right triangle).

### Interactive Widget Idea: `PythagoreanExplorer`
* **Interaction**: Drag vertices of a right triangle. Shows three squares on sides $a$, $b$, and $c$. Counts unit squares within them to verify $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$.
* **Quiz**: "Construct a right triangle with side lengths 6 and 8. What is the area of the square on the hypotenuse?"

---

## 6. Statistics & Probability

### Core Concept: Measures of Center and Compound Events
- **Level 1 (Intuition)**: Dragging values on a dot plot to see how mean, median, and mode shift.
- **Level 2 (Procedural)**: Calculating mean, median, mode, and range.
- **Level 3 (Challenge)**: Understanding outliers and basic independent probability trees.

### Interactive Widget Idea: `DotPlotStats`
* **Interaction**: User drags data points up/down on a chart. Horizontal lines for Mean and Median update instantly.
* **Quiz**: "Add a single outlier data point that shifts the mean significantly but leaves the median unchanged."

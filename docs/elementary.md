# Elementary School Curriculum (Grades 1-6) - AsahFikir

This document details the modules, interactive concepts, and adaptive quiz ideas for the **Elementary School** track.

---

## 1. Arithmetic Fundamentals

### Core Concept: Visualizing Quantities and Operations
- **Level 1 (Intuition)**: The "Balance Scale". Adding/subtracting blocks on the left/right balance beam.
- **Level 2 (Procedural)**: Column addition/subtraction with visual carries.
- **Level 3 (Challenge)**: Number puzzles (e.g., fill in missing operators, sum wheels).

### Interactive Widget Idea: `BalanceScale`
* **Interaction**: User drags weights (units of 1, 5, 10) onto a virtual scale to balance it, illustrating equations like $3 + x = 7$.
* **Quiz**: "If 1 apple balances with 3 cherries, and 1 cherry balances with 2 grapes, how many grapes balance 1 apple?"

---

## 2. Fractions

### Core Concept: Parts of a Whole and Equivalent Fractions
- **Level 1 (Intuition)**: Interactive circle/bar pie slices.
- **Level 2 (Procedural)**: Adding fractions with visual common denominators.
- **Level 3 (Challenge)**: Fraction comparisons with unequal parts.

### Interactive Widget Idea: `FractionVisualizer`
* **Interaction**: User adjusts numerator and denominator sliders. A pie chart or bar fills dynamically. Two shapes are shown side-by-side to show equivalence (e.g. $2/4$ and $4/8$).
* **Quiz**: "Slide the controls to make the two circles shaded identically, then write down the equivalent fraction."

---

## 3. Decimals

### Core Concept: Base-10 Place Values and Grids
- **Level 1 (Intuition)**: $10 \times 10$ grids where shading 1 cell equals $0.01$, a row equals $0.1$.
- **Level 2 (Procedural)**: Matching decimals to fractions (e.g., $0.25 \leftrightarrow 1/4$).
- **Level 3 (Challenge)**: Fast addition of decimal parts in a runner game setting.

### Interactive Widget Idea: `DecimalGrid`
* **Interaction**: A $10 \times 10$ grid. Clicking cells toggles shading. A text output displays the decimal value, percent, and fraction.
* **Quiz**: "Shade $0.34$ of the grid. Now shade an additional $0.45$. What decimal represents the total shaded area?"

---

## 4. Measurement & Geometry

### Core Concept: Area, Perimeter, and Units
- **Level 1 (Intuition)**: Geoboard grid. User stretches rubber bands to create shapes and counts unit squares.
- **Level 2 (Procedural)**: Calculating area of composite shapes by partitioning them into rectangles.
- **Level 3 (Challenge)**: Maximize area given a fixed perimeter.

### Interactive Widget Idea: `GeoboardArea`
* **Interaction**: Grid of pegs. User clicks and drags vertices to form polygons. The widget dynamically displays perimeter and counts interior unit blocks.
* **Quiz**: "Create a shape with a perimeter of 12 units. What is the maximum area you can achieve?"

---

## 5. Data & Probability

### Core Concept: Frequency, Bar Charts, and Spinners
- **Level 1 (Intuition)**: Interactive spinner. Spin 10 times, record results, and plot a bar chart dynamically.
- **Level 2 (Procedural)**: Calculating basic probability (e.g., likelihood of landing on blue).
- **Level 3 (Challenge)**: The Monty Hall problem simplified or expected values with colored marbles in a jar.

### Interactive Widget Idea: `ProbabilitySpinner`
* **Interaction**: Customize colors of a wheel (e.g., 3 red, 1 blue), spin it multiple times in fast-forward, and observe empirical vs. theoretical probability.
* **Quiz**: "Change the spinner sections so that landing on Yellow is three times as likely as landing on Red."

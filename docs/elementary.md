## 1. Arithmetic Fundamentals

### Core Concept: Visualizing Quantities and Operations
- **Level 1 (Intuition)**: The "Balance Scale". Adding/subtracting blocks on the left/right balance beam.
- **Level 2 (Procedural)**: Column addition/subtraction with visual carries.
- **Level 3 (Challenge)**: Number puzzles (e.g., fill in missing operators, sum wheels).

### Interactive Widget Idea: `BalanceScale`
* **Interaction**: User drags weights (units of 1, 5, 10) onto a virtual scale to balance it, illustrating equations like $3 + x = 7$.

### Graded Quiz Questions (At least 5 per level)

#### Level 1: Concrete Balancing & Addition
1. **Question 1**: Solve for $x$ on a scale: left side has mystery box $x$ and a 3-unit block, right side has an 8-unit block.
   - *Step-by-step*: 
     1. Identify the balance equation: $x + 3 = 8$.
     2. Subtract 3 units from the left side of the scale.
     3. To keep the scale balanced, subtract 3 units from the right side.
     4. This leaves the mystery box $x$ by itself, balancing with a 5-unit block ($x = 5$).
2. **Question 2**: Left side has one box $x$ and a 2-unit block. Right side has a 7-unit block. What is $x$?
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Set up equation: $x + 2 = 7$.
     2. Remove 2 units from both sides.
     3. Result is $x = 5$.
3. **Question 3**: Left side has one box $x$ and a 5-unit block. Right side has a 9-unit block. What is $x$?
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. $x + 5 = 9$.
     2. Subtract 5 units from both sides.
     3. Result is $x = 4$.
4. **Question 4**: Left side has one box $x$ and a 1-unit block. Right side has a 10-unit block. What is $x$?
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. $x + 1 = 10$.
     2. Subtract 1 unit from both sides.
     3. Result is $x = 9$.
5. **Question 5**: Left side has one box $x$ and a 6-unit block. Right side has an 8-unit block. What is $x$?
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. $x + 6 = 8$.
     2. Subtract 6 units from both sides.
     3. Result is $x = 2$.

#### Level 2: Double Unknowns & Inverse Operations
1. **Question 6**: Left side has two identical boxes $x$ and a 2-unit block. Right side has a 12-unit block. Solve for one box $x$.
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Set up equation: $2x + 2 = 12$.
     2. Remove the 2-unit block from both sides, leaving $2x = 10$.
     3. Divide the remaining 10 units equally between the 2 boxes.
     4. Each box $x$ weighs 5 units ($x = 5$).
2. **Question 7**: Solve for $x$: $2x + 4 = 14$.
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. $2x + 4 = 14$.
     2. Subtract 4 from both sides to isolate the $2x$: $2x = 10$.
     3. Divide both sides by 2 to find $x$: $x = 5$.
3. **Question 8**: Solve for $x$: $3x + 1 = 10$.
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Subtract 1 from both sides: $3x = 9$.
     2. Divide by 3: $x = 3$.
4. **Question 9**: Solve for $x$: $2x + 6 = 16$.
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Subtract 6 from both sides: $2x = 10$.
     2. Divide by 2: $x = 5$.
5. **Question 10**: Solve for $x$: $3x + 3 = 15$.
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Subtract 3 from both sides: $3x = 12$.
     2. Divide by 3: $x = 4$.

#### Level 3: Dual-Side Variables & Operations
1. **Question 11**: Solve for $x$ on a scale: left has one box $x$ and a 7-unit block; right has two boxes $x$ and a 3-unit block.
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Write the equation: $x + 7 = 2x + 3$.
     2. Simplify by removing one box $x$ from both sides, leaving $7 = x + 3$.
     3. Subtract 3 units from both sides.
     4. This leaves $4 = x$, so the mystery box weighs 4.
2. **Question 12**: Solve for $x$: $2x + 5 = 3x + 2$.
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Subtract $2x$ from both sides: $5 = x + 2$.
     2. Subtract 2 from both sides: $x = 3$.
3. **Question 13**: Solve for $x$: $x + 10 = 2x + 6$.
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Subtract $x$ from both sides: $10 = x + 6$.
     2. Subtract 6 from both sides: $x = 4$.
4. **Question 14**: Solve for $x$: $2x + 8 = 3x + 4$.
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Subtract $2x$ from both sides: $8 = x + 4$.
     2. Subtract 4 from both sides: $x = 4$.
5. **Question 15**: Solve for $x$: $x + 12 = 3x + 6$.
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Subtract $x$ from both sides: $12 = 2x + 6$.
     2. Subtract 6 from both sides: $6 = 2x$.
     3. Divide by 2: $x = 3$.

## 2. Fractions

### Core Concept: Parts of a Whole and Equivalent Fractions
- **Level 1 (Intuition)**: Interactive circle/bar pie slices.
- **Level 2 (Procedural)**: Adding fractions with visual common denominators.
- **Level 3 (Challenge)**: Fraction comparisons with unequal parts.

### Interactive Widget Idea: `FractionVisualizer`
* **Interaction**: User adjusts numerator and denominator sliders. A pie chart or bar fills dynamically. Two shapes are shown side-by-side to show equivalence.

### Graded Quiz Questions (At least 5 per level)

#### Level 1: Visual Fraction Identification
1. **Question 1**: What fraction of the circle is shaded when you select 3 parts out of 4?
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Look at the circle divided into 4 total equal slices.
     2. Count the shaded slices: there are 3.
     3. Write the numerator (top number) as 3 and the denominator (bottom number) as 4.
     4. The fraction is $3/4$.
2. **Question 2**: What fraction of a circle is shaded when you select 1 part out of 2?
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Denominator is total parts = 2.
     2. Numerator is shaded parts = 1.
     3. The fraction is $1/2$.
3. **Question 3**: A rectangle is divided into 5 equal columns, and 2 are shaded. What is the fraction?
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Total divisions = 5.
     2. Shaded divisions = 2.
     3. The fraction is $2/5$.
4. **Question 4**: Shading 5 slices out of 6 gives what fraction?
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Numerator is 5.
     2. Denominator is 6.
     3. The fraction is $5/6$.
5. **Question 5**: What fraction represents a whole circle shaded when it's cut into 3 slices?
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. If all 3 slices of a 3-slice circle are shaded, the numerator is 3 and denominator is 3.
     2. The fraction is $3/3$ (which equals 1).

#### Level 2: Equivalent Fractions
1. **Question 6**: Which of the following is equivalent to $1/3$?
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Understand equivalent fractions represent the same visual portion.
     2. Multiply both the numerator and denominator by the same integer.
     3. For $1/3$, multiply by 2: $(1 \times 2) / (3 \times 2) = 2/6$.
     4. Thus, $2/6$ is equivalent to $1/3$.
2. **Question 7**: Find a fraction equivalent to $2/4$ with a denominator of 8.
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Compare denominators: to go from 4 to 8, we multiply by 2.
     2. Multiply the numerator by the same number: $2 \times 2 = 4$.
     3. The equivalent fraction is $4/8$.
3. **Question 8**: Which fraction is equivalent to $3/5$?
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Multiply top and bottom by 2: $3 \times 2 = 6$ and $5 \times 2 = 10$.
     2. The equivalent fraction is $6/10$.
4. **Question 9**: Simplify the fraction $4/12$.
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Find the greatest common factor (GCF) of 4 and 12, which is 4.
     2. Divide the numerator: $4 / 4 = 1$.
     3. Divide the denominator: $12 / 4 = 3$.
     4. The simplified fraction is $1/3$.
5. **Question 10**: Which fraction is equivalent to $2/3$?
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Multiply top and bottom by 3: $2 \times 3 = 6$ and $3 \times 3 = 9$.
     2. The equivalent fraction is $6/9$.

#### Level 3: Comparing Fractions
1. **Question 11**: Which fraction is larger: $3/5$ or $2/3$?
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. To compare fractions with different denominators, find a common denominator.
     2. The least common multiple of 5 and 3 is 15.
     3. Convert $3/5 \rightarrow (3 \times 3)/(5 \times 3) = 9/15$.
     4. Convert $2/3 \rightarrow (2 \times 5)/(3 \times 5) = 10/15$.
     5. Since $10/15 > 9/15$, $2/3$ is larger than $3/5$.
2. **Question 12**: Compare $1/2$ and $2/5$. Which is larger?
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Common denominator is 10.
     2. $1/2 = 5/10$.
     3. $2/5 = 4/10$.
     4. Since $5/10 > 4/10$, $1/2$ is larger.
3. **Question 13**: Which is smaller: $3/4$ or $4/5$?
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Common denominator is 20.
     2. $3/4 = 15/20$.
     3. $4/5 = 16/20$.
     4. Since $15/20 < 16/20$, $3/4$ is smaller.
4. **Question 14**: Order these from smallest to largest: $1/3$, $1/4$, $1/2$.
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. With identical numerators, a larger denominator means smaller slices.
     2. 4 is the largest denominator, so $1/4$ is the smallest.
     3. 2 is the smallest denominator, so $1/2$ is the largest.
     4. The order is: $1/4 < 1/3 < 1/2$.
5. **Question 15**: Is $5/8$ larger or smaller than $3/4$?
   - *Step-by-step*:
     1. Convert $3/4$ to have a denominator of 8: $3/4 = 6/8$.
     2. Compare: $5/8$ vs $6/8$.
     3. Since $5/8 < 6/8$, $5/8$ is smaller than $3/4$.
